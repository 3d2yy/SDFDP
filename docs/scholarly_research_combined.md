# Comprehensive Annotated Bibliography: Advancements in UHF Partial Discharge Detection (2024–2026)

This document provides a comprehensive synthesis of recent scholarly articles and specialized research regarding Ultra-High Frequency (UHF) sensors and Partial Discharge (PD) detection. It seamlessly integrates basic summaries, key findings, and in-depth annotations to provide a complete overview of the state-of-the-art in the field. It also highlights notable research contributions from the Universidad Tecnológica de Panamá (UTP).

## Part I: 10 Most Recent Scholarly Articles (2024–2026)

### 1. Research on GIS Partial Discharge Detection Based on UHF Sensors
**Date:** February 19, 2026 | **Source:** ResearchGate
* **Summary:** Explores the complex dynamics of intermittent partial discharges triggered by metal contamination within Gas-Insulated Switchgear (GIS) environments.
* **Key Findings:** Investigates the behavioral change laws of intermittent discharge. Using a dual-methodological approach (Pulse Current and UHF), the researchers mapped the correlation between physical discharge and electromagnetic radiation, demonstrating the superior capability of UHF sensors to capture transient electromagnetic waves during intermittent discharge cycles.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Confirms that UHF sensors are highly effective at capturing transient electromagnetic waves, providing a more reliable diagnostic tool for early-stage defect identification before catastrophic insulation failure occurs.
* **Citation:** X. Zhang et al., "Research on GIS Partial Discharge Detection Based on UHF Sensors," _Sensors & Transducers_, vol. 301, no. 2, pp. 45–58, Feb. 19, 2026. [Online]. Available: ResearchGate.

### 2. Classification and Recognition of Ultra-High-Frequency Partial Discharge Signals Based on Adaptive Hybrid Attention Fusion Network
**Date:** February 02, 2026 | **Source:** MDPI
* **Summary:** Introduces a novel, state-of-the-art deep learning architecture specifically designed for the classification of complex UHF partial discharge signals.
* **Key Findings:** Proposes a sophisticated dual-flow network architecture that utilizes a ResNet branch to extract fine-grained local spatial features, and a Swin Transformer branch to capture global dependencies within the signal data. This synergistic approach effectively filters out environmental noise.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Achieves an unprecedented detection accuracy of 99.58% for various transformer insulation defects, significantly outperforming traditional machine learning classifiers and reducing the margin of error in diagnostic systems.
* **Citation:** L. Wang and J. Chen, "Classification and Recognition of Ultra-High-Frequency Partial Discharge Signals Based on Adaptive Hybrid Attention Fusion Network," _Applied Sciences_, vol. 16, no. 3, Art. no. 1479, Feb. 2, 2026. doi: 10.3390/app16031479.

### 3. Research on UAV Ultra-High Frequency Partial Discharge Detection
**Date:** January 04, 2026 | **Source:** ResearchGate (IEEE Access)
* **Summary:** Details the implementation of an online, Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) based monitoring system optimized for inspecting hard-to-reach power transformers and overhead infrastructure.
* **Key Findings:** Integrates lightweight UHF sensors onto UAVs, utilizing peak envelope detection algorithms tailored for an airborne platform to enable efficient remote sensing.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Demonstrated high sensitivity and superior interference suppression against background corona and communication signals during rigorous field tests. This proves the viability and represents a significant leap in the mobility and accessibility of drone-based inspections for high-voltage infrastructure.
* **Citation:** H. Liu et al., "Research on UAV Ultra-High Frequency Partial Discharge Detection," _IEEE Access_, Early Access, Jan. 4, 2026. [Online]. Available: ResearchGate.

### 4. Advanced Signal Processing Methods for Partial Discharge Analysis: A Comprehensive Review
**Date:** December 01, 2025 | **Source:** PMC (NCBI)
* **Summary:** Provides a critical and systematic evaluation of contemporary signal processing techniques used to analyze complex, non-stationary partial discharge signals.
* **Key Findings:** Deeply contrasts traditional time-frequency analysis tools, such as Wavelet Transforms (WT) and Hilbert-Huang Transforms (HHT), against emerging AI-driven denoising frameworks. It highlights the respective strengths and limitations in handling highly noisy industrial environments.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Serves as a foundational guide and strategic roadmap for researchers to select the optimal processing pipeline based on specific hardware constraints and monitoring objectives.
* **Citation:** Y. Kim and T. Tanaka, "Advanced Signal Processing Methods for Partial Discharge Analysis: A Comprehensive Review," _Journal of Electrical Engineering & Technology_, vol. 20, no. 6, pp. 1102–1120, Dec. 2025. [Online]. Available: PMC12694323.

### 5. Research on Partial Discharge Signal Recognition and Localization Based on Multi-Scale Feature Fusion
**Date:** November 21, 2025 | **Source:** PMC (NCBI)
* **Summary:** Tackles the challenging problem of identifying and localizing partial discharges when multiple defect sources are active simultaneously in complex high-voltage equipment.
* **Key Findings:** Establishes a synergistic, multi-scale feature fusion framework that integrates adaptive signal processing techniques with an attention-based deep learning module. The system dynamically weights the most relevant features across different scales to reliably isolate overlapping signals.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Achieved a robust 96.2% classification accuracy for multi-source defect scenarios, offering a highly reliable solution for complex fault environments.
* **Citation:** Z. Zhao et al., "Research on Partial Discharge Signal Recognition and Localization Based on Multi-Scale Feature Fusion," _Energies_, vol. 18, no. 22, Nov. 2025. [Online]. Available: PMC12633952.

### 6. Research on a Degradation Identification Method for GIS UHF Sensors
**Date:** November 10, 2025 | **Source:** MDPI
* **Summary:** Shifts the focus from power equipment to the diagnostic sensors themselves by addressing the physical degradation of UHF sensors due to continuous exposure to harsh, high-field GIS environments.
* **Key Findings:** Develops a pioneering, in-situ sensitivity identification procedure to quantify how long-term service affects sensor accuracy and performance degradation without requiring sensor removal.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Enhances the reliability of GIS monitoring by allowing operators to calibrate systems dynamically, ensuring that data interpretation accounts for sensor aging and sensitivity loss throughout the operational lifecycle.
* **Citation:** S. Gupta and R. Sharma, "Research on a Degradation Identification Method for GIS UHF Sensors," _Sensors_, vol. 25, no. 22, Art. no. 6860, Nov. 2025. doi: 10.3390/s25226860.

### 7. UHF Signal Processing and Pattern Recognition of Partial Discharge in GIS Using Chromatic Methodology
**Date:** October 16, 2025 | **Source:** ResearchGate
* **Summary:** Proposes a highly innovative, cross-disciplinary "chromatic monitoring" technique adapted from color science and human visual perception for PD pattern recognition.
* **Key Findings:** Instead of relying on traditional Phase-Resolved Partial Discharge (PRPD) patterns, this method processes raw UHF signals by mapping their complex spectral characteristics into sparse, three-dimensional parameters: hue, lightness, and saturation (HLS).
* **Impact/Contribution:** Significantly reduces computational overhead while creating intuitive, distinct visual clusters for different types of GIS defects. This provides a new mathematical framework making high-dimensional data easier to classify and facilitating faster automated diagnosis.
* **Citation:** M. J. Smith et al., "UHF Signal Processing and Pattern Recognition of Partial Discharge in GIS Using Chromatic Methodology," _IEEE Transactions on Dielectrics and Electrical Insulation_, vol. 32, no. 5, pp. 1540–1548, Oct. 2025. [Online]. Available: ResearchGate.

### 8. Design and Evaluation of an Integrated Ultra‐High Frequency and Optical Sensor
**Date:** April 16, 2025 | **Source:** IET High Voltage
* **Summary:** Explores the advantages of multi-physics sensor fusion by designing and evaluating a novel, hybrid sensor platform for 500 kV GIS systems.
* **Key Findings:** The integrated sensor captures and applies joint, synchronous analysis to both Ultra-High Frequency electromagnetic waves and optical emissions simultaneously.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Dramatically reduces false positive rates and improves detection reliability for complex defects. It proves exceptionally reliable for distinguishing critical issues like free-moving metal particles and floating potential defects from benign background noise compared to single-mode sensing.
* **Citation:** T. Wu et al., "Design and Evaluation of an Integrated Ultra‐High Frequency and Optical Sensor," _High Voltage_, vol. 10, no. 2, pp. 88–97, Apr. 2025. doi: 10.1049/hve2.12450.

### 9. Research on State Diagnosis Methods of UHF Partial Discharge Sensors Based on Improved ViT
**Date:** December 02, 2024 | **Source:** MDPI
* **Summary:** Applies cutting-edge computer vision architecture to the domain of sensor health monitoring by transforming time-series UHF sensor data into 2D representations.
* **Key Findings:** Utilizes an improved Vision Transformer (ViT) enhanced with a sliding window mechanism. This allows the model to focus on specific, critical local temporal features within the UHF spectrograms that traditional CNNs or standard transformers might miss.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Reached a highly accurate automated diagnostic capability (97.6%) in identifying sensor health, faults, and discharge types before they compromise the broader GIS monitoring network.
* **Citation:** J. Park et al., "Research on State Diagnosis Methods of UHF Partial Discharge Sensors Based on Improved ViT," _Applied Sciences_, vol. 14, no. 23, Art. no. 11214, Dec. 2024. doi: 10.3390/app142311214.

### 10. Classification of Partial Discharge Sources in Ultra-High Frequency Range Using Envelope Detection
**Date:** June 19, 2024 | **Source:** MDPI
* **Summary:** Focuses on democratizing PD monitoring through cost-effective hardware design and efficient edge computing to reduce hardware requirements.
* **Key Findings:** Presents a streamlined methodology utilizing a custom-designed, low-cost printed UHF monopole antenna paired with an analog envelope detection circuit. Extracting the envelope before digitization drastically lowers the required high-speed sampling rate and hardware costs.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Coupled with a lightweight machine learning algorithm, the system maintains a robust classification accuracy of over 84%, providing a cost-effective alternative for widespread, continuous monitoring in broader distribution networks.
* **Citation:** A. Rodriguez et al., "Classification of Partial Discharge Sources in Ultra-High Frequency Range Using Envelope Detection," _Electronics_, vol. 13, no. 12, Art. no. 2399, Jun. 2024. doi: 10.3390/electronics13122399.

## Part II: Research Conducted in Panama (UTP)

Researchers from Panamanian institutions, particularly the Universidad Tecnológica de Panamá (UTP), have contributed significantly to the specialized areas of UHF-PD source separation and environmental spectral analysis.

### 11. Detection of Partial Discharge Sources Using UHF Sensors and Independent Component Analysis
**Source:** PubMed/PMC
* **Context:** A foundational study addressing the complex "cocktail party problem" of partial discharge monitoring: separating multiple, overlapping PD signals occurring simultaneously.
* **Methodology & Findings:** Proposes the application of an Independent Component Analysis (ICA) algorithm to blind-source separate the mixed UHF signals captured by multiple sensors. The efficacy and accuracy of the separated signal profiles were rigorously validated using Support Vector Machines (SVM) and class mean similarity metrics.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Proves that individual defect sources can be accurately isolated and identified even in highly contaminated signal environments.
* **Citation:** F. Alvarez, G. Robles, and J. M. Robles, "Detection of Partial Discharge Sources Using UHF Sensors and Independent Component Analysis," _Sensors (Basel)_, vol. 17, no. 11, Nov. 2017. [Online]. Available: PMC5712867.

### 12. Variation in the Spectral Content of UHF PD Signals Due to the Presence of Obstacles
**Source:** IEEE Xplore
* **Context:** An essential investigation into the real-world physical limitations of UHF-based PD detection, focusing on the physics of signal propagation within large power apparatus.
* **Methodology & Findings:** Meticulously characterizes how the internal geometry and physical obstacles within a power transformer (such as intricate copper windings and core structural supports) attenuate, reflect, and modify the high-frequency spectral content of propagating PD signals.
* **Impact/Contribution:** Alerts practitioners to signal distortion issues. By quantifying these distortions, the study provides crucial correction factors necessary for improving the accuracy of both source localization algorithms and the subsequent classification of defect types in complex, non-ideal measurement environments.
* **Citation:** D. Gonzalez and F. Alvarez, "Variation in the Spectral Content of UHF PD Signals Due to the Presence of Obstacles," in _2023 IEEE 4th International Conference on Dielectrics (ICD)_, July 2023, pp. 1-4. doi: 10.1109/ICD46981.2023.10227907.
