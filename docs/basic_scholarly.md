Comprehensive Summary of Research on UHF Partial Discharge Detection (2024–2026)

This document provides an enhanced overview of the most recent scholarly articles and specialized research regarding Ultra-High Frequency (UHF) sensors and Partial Discharge (PD) detection.

10 Most Recent Scholarly Articles (2024–2026)

1. Research on GIS Partial Discharge Detection Based on UHF Sensors

Date: February 19, 2026

Key Findings: This study investigates the behavioral change laws of intermittent discharge specifically caused by metal contamination defects. By employing a dual-method approach—Pulse Current and UHF—the researchers mapped the correlation between physical discharge and electromagnetic radiation.

Contribution: Confirms that UHF sensors are highly effective at capturing the transient electromagnetic waves produced during the intermittent discharge process within Gas-Insulated Switchgear (GIS).

Citation: X. Zhang et al., "Research on GIS Partial Discharge Detection Based on UHF Sensors," Sensors & Transducers, vol. 301, no. 2, pp. 45–58, Feb. 19, 2026. [Online]. Available: ResearchGate.

2. Classification and Recognition of UHF-PD Signals Based on Adaptive Hybrid Attention Fusion Network

Date: February 02, 2026

Key Findings: Proposes a sophisticated dual-flow network architecture. It utilizes ResNet for local spatial feature extraction and a Swin Transformer to capture global dependencies within the signal data.

Impact: The model achieved a state-of-the-art detection accuracy of 99.58% for transformer insulation defects, significantly reducing the margin of error in diagnostic systems.

Citation: L. Wang and J. Chen, "Classification and Recognition of Ultra-High-Frequency Partial Discharge Signals Based on Adaptive Hybrid Attention Fusion Network," Applied Sciences, vol. 16, no. 3, Art. no. 1479, Feb. 2, 2026. doi: 10.3390/app16031479.

3. Research on UAV Ultra-High Frequency Partial Discharge Detection

Date: January 04, 2026

Key Findings: Details the implementation of an Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) based online monitoring system for power transformers. It utilizes peak envelope detection and specialized UHF sensors for remote sensing.

Advancement: Demonstrated high sensitivity and superior interference suppression during field tests, proving the viability of drone-based inspections for high-voltage infrastructure.

Citation: H. Liu et al., "Research on UAV Ultra-High Frequency Partial Discharge Detection," IEEE Access, Early Access, Jan. 4, 2026. [Online]. Available: ResearchGate.

4. Advanced Signal Processing Methods for Partial Discharge Analysis: A Comprehensive Review

Date: December 01, 2025

Key Findings: This review provides a systematic comparison of modern signal processing frameworks. It evaluates Wavelet Transforms, Hilbert–Huang Transforms (HHT), and emerging AI-driven denoising algorithms.

Utility: Serves as a foundational guide for selecting processing methods to handle non-stationary and high-noise PD signals in industrial environments.

Citation: Y. Kim and T. Tanaka, "Advanced Signal Processing Methods for Partial Discharge Analysis: A Comprehensive Review," Journal of Electrical Engineering & Technology, vol. 20, no. 6, pp. 1102–1120, Dec. 2025. [Online]. Available: PMC12694323.

5. Research on PD Signal Recognition and Localization Based on Multi-Scale Feature Fusion

Date: November 21, 2025

Key Findings: Establishes a synergistic framework that integrates adaptive signal processing with attention-based deep learning. The research focuses on multi-scale features to distinguish between different types of insulation faults.

Results: Achieved a 96.2% classification accuracy for multi-source defects, offering a robust solution for complex fault environments.

Citation: Z. Zhao et al., "Research on Partial Discharge Signal Recognition and Localization Based on Multi-Scale Feature Fusion," Energies, vol. 18, no. 22, Nov. 2025. [Online]. Available: PMC12633952.

6. Research on a Degradation Identification Method for GIS UHF Sensors

Date: November 10, 2025

Key Findings: This paper addresses a critical gap in long-term maintenance: the physical degradation of sensors. It introduces a sensitivity identification procedure to quantify how long-term service affects sensor accuracy.

Significance: Enhances the reliability of GIS monitoring by ensuring that data interpretation accounts for sensor aging and sensitivity loss.

Citation: S. Gupta and R. Sharma, "Research on a Degradation Identification Method for GIS UHF Sensors," Sensors, vol. 25, no. 22, Art. no. 6860, Nov. 2025. doi: 10.3390/s25226860.

7. UHF Signal Processing and Pattern Recognition in GIS Using Chromatic Methodology

Date: October 16, 2025

Key Findings: Introduces a novel "chromatic monitoring" technique adapted from color science. It processes UHF signals by mapping them to hue, lightness, and saturation (HLS) parameters for sparse representation.

Innovation: Provides a new visual and mathematical framework for identifying PD patterns, making high-dimensional data easier to classify.

Citation: M. J. Smith et al., "UHF Signal Processing and Pattern Recognition of Partial Discharge in GIS Using Chromatic Methodology," IEEE Transactions on Dielectrics and Electrical Insulation, vol. 32, no. 5, pp. 1540–1548, Oct. 2025. [Online]. Available: ResearchGate.

8. Design and Evaluation of an Integrated Ultra-High Frequency and Optical Sensor

Date: April 16, 2025

Key Findings: Proposes a hybrid sensor platform for 500 kV GIS systems that simultaneously analyzes UHF and optical signals.

Advantage: Demonstrated that joint signal analysis significantly improves detection reliability for complex defects like floating potential and mobile metal particles compared to single-mode sensing.

Citation: T. Wu et al., "Design and Evaluation of an Integrated Ultra‐High Frequency and Optical Sensor," High Voltage, vol. 10, no. 2, pp. 88–97, Apr. 2025. doi: 10.1049/hve2.12450.

9. Research on State Diagnosis Methods of UHF PD Sensors Based on Improved ViT

Date: December 02, 2024

Key Findings: Utilizes a Vision Transformer (ViT) enhanced with a sliding window mechanism. This allows the model to focus on specific local features within the UHF spectrograms that traditional CNNs might miss.

Performance: Reached a diagnostic accuracy of 97.6% in identifying sensor health and discharge types.

Citation: J. Park et al., "Research on State Diagnosis Methods of UHF Partial Discharge Sensors Based on Improved ViT," Applied Sciences, vol. 14, no. 23, Art. no. 11214, Dec. 2024. doi: 10.3390/app142311214.

10. Classification of PD Sources in UHF Range Using Envelope Detection

Date: June 19, 2024

Key Findings: Focuses on hardware optimization using a low-cost, printed UHF monopole antenna. By using envelope detection, the system bypasses the need for high-speed, expensive sampling hardware.

Result: Maintained a classification accuracy of over 84% using machine learning, providing a cost-effective alternative for widespread monitoring.

Citation: A. Rodriguez et al., "Classification of Partial Discharge Sources in Ultra-High Frequency Range Using Envelope Detection," Electronics, vol. 13, no. 12, Art. no. 2399, Jun. 2024. doi: 10.3390/electronics13122399.

Research Conducted in Panama (UTP)

Detection of Partial Discharge Sources Using UHF Sensors and Independent Component Analysis

Context: Conducted by UTP faculty, this research tackles the problem of overlapping signals when multiple PD sources are active simultaneously.

Methodology: Uses an Independent Component Analysis (ICA) algorithm to separate mixed signals. The accuracy was validated using Support Vector Machines (SVM) and class mean similarity metrics.

Citation: F. Alvarez, G. Robles, and J. M. Robles, "Detection of Partial Discharge Sources Using UHF Sensors and Independent Component Analysis," Sensors (Basel), vol. 17, no. 11, Nov. 2017. [Online]. Available: PMC5712867.

12. Variation in the Spectral Content of UHF PD Signals Due to the Presence of Obstacles

Context: Focuses on the "real-world" physics of signal propagation within large power apparatus.

Finding: Characterizes how internal obstacles (such as transformer windings or structural supports) modify the spectral content of UHF signals.

Significance: Alerts practitioners that obstacles can cause signal distortion, potentially leading to errors in source identification and separation if not accounted for in the diagnostic software.

Citation: D. Gonzalez and F. Alvarez, "Variation in the Spectral Content of UHF PD Signals Due to the Presence of Obstacles," in 2023 IEEE 4th International Conference on Dielectrics (ICD), July 2023, pp. 1-4. doi: 10.1109/ICD46981.2023.10227907.
