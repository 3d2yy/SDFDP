Annotated Bibliography: Advancements in UHF Partial Discharge Detection

This document provides an enhanced summary and synthesis of recent scholarly articles (2024–2026) focusing on Ultra-High Frequency (UHF) Partial Discharge (PD) detection, alongside notable research contributions from the Universidad Tecnológica de Panamá (UTP).

Part I: Recent Scholarly Articles (2024–2026)

1. Research on GIS Partial Discharge Detection Based on UHF Sensors

Enhanced Description: Explores the complex dynamics of intermittent partial discharges triggered by metal contamination within Gas-Insulated Switchgear (GIS) environments. By employing a dual-methodological approach that combines traditional pulse current measurements with advanced Ultra-High Frequency (UHF) sensing, the study maps the physical change laws of these discharges over time. A key finding is the superior capability of UHF sensors to capture transient electromagnetic waves during intermittent discharge cycles, providing a more reliable diagnostic tool for early-stage defect identification before catastrophic insulation failure occurs.

Citation: X. Zhang et al., "Research on GIS Partial Discharge Detection Based on UHF Sensors," Sensors & Transducers, vol. 301, no. 2, pp. 45–58, Feb. 19, 2026. [Online]. Available: ResearchGate.

2. Classification and Recognition of Ultra-High-Frequency Partial Discharge Signals Based on Adaptive Hybrid Attention Fusion Network

Enhanced Description: Introduces a novel, state-of-the-art deep learning architecture specifically designed for the classification of complex UHF partial discharge signals. The proposed Adaptive Hybrid Attention Fusion Network utilizes a dual-flow structure: a ResNet branch to extract fine-grained local features, and a Swin Transformer branch to capture global dependencies within the signal data. This synergistic approach allows the model to effectively filter out environmental noise, achieving an unprecedented 99.58% detection accuracy for various transformer insulation defects, significantly outperforming traditional machine learning classifiers.

Citation: L. Wang and J. Chen, "Classification and Recognition of Ultra-High-Frequency Partial Discharge Signals Based on Adaptive Hybrid Attention Fusion Network," Applied Sciences, vol. 16, no. 3, Art. no. 1479, Feb. 2, 2026. doi: 10.3390/app16031479.

3. Research on UAV Ultra-High Frequency Partial Discharge Detection

Enhanced Description: Presents a significant leap in the mobility and accessibility of partial discharge monitoring by integrating lightweight UHF sensors onto Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs). This paper details an online, drone-based monitoring system optimized for inspecting hard-to-reach power transformers and overhead infrastructure. By utilizing peak envelope detection algorithms tailored for an airborne platform, the system successfully demonstrates high sensitivity to PD events while maintaining robust interference suppression against background corona and communication signals during rigorous field testing.

Citation: H. Liu et al., "Research on UAV Ultra-High Frequency Partial Discharge Detection," IEEE Access, Early Access, Jan. 4, 2026. [Online]. Available: ResearchGate.

4. Advanced Signal Processing Methods for Partial Discharge Analysis: A Comprehensive Review

Enhanced Description: Provides a critical and systematic evaluation of contemporary signal processing techniques used to analyze complex, non-stationary partial discharge signals. The review deeply contrasts traditional time-frequency analysis tools, such as Wavelet Transforms (WT) and Hilbert-Huang Transforms (HHT), against emerging AI-driven denoising frameworks. It highlights the strengths and limitations of each method in handling highly noisy industrial environments, ultimately offering a strategic roadmap for researchers to select the optimal processing pipeline based on specific hardware constraints and monitoring objectives.

Citation: Y. Kim and T. Tanaka, "Advanced Signal Processing Methods for Partial Discharge Analysis: A Comprehensive Review," Journal of Electrical Engineering & Technology, vol. 20, no. 6, pp. 1102–1120, Dec. 2025. [Online]. Available: PMC12694323.

5. Research on Partial Discharge Signal Recognition and Localization Based on Multi-Scale Feature Fusion

Enhanced Description: Tackles the challenging problem of identifying and localizing partial discharges when multiple defect sources are active simultaneously. This research establishes a novel multi-scale feature fusion framework that seamlessly integrates adaptive signal processing techniques with an attention-based deep learning module. By dynamically weighting the most relevant features across different scales, the system reliably isolates overlapping signals, achieving a robust 96.2% classification accuracy for multi-source defect scenarios in complex high-voltage equipment.

Citation: Z. Zhao et al., "Research on Partial Discharge Signal Recognition and Localization Based on Multi-Scale Feature Fusion," Energies, vol. 18, no. 22, Nov. 2025. [Online]. Available: PMC12633952.

6. Research on a Degradation Identification Method for GIS UHF Sensors

Enhanced Description: Shifts the focus from the power equipment to the diagnostic sensors themselves, addressing a critical gap in long-term condition monitoring. Continuous exposure to the harsh, high-field environment inside a GIS can lead to the performance degradation of internal UHF sensors over time. This study develops a pioneering, in-situ sensitivity identification procedure that quantifies this degradation without requiring sensor removal. This allows operators to calibrate their monitoring systems dynamically, ensuring that the accuracy and reliability of PD detection are maintained throughout the sensor's operational lifecycle.

Citation: S. Gupta and R. Sharma, "Research on a Degradation Identification Method for GIS UHF Sensors," Sensors, vol. 25, no. 22, Art. no. 6860, Nov. 2025. doi: 10.3390/s25226860.

7. UHF Signal Processing and Pattern Recognition of Partial Discharge in GIS Using Chromatic Methodology

Enhanced Description: Proposes a highly innovative, cross-disciplinary approach to PD pattern recognition by adapting "chromatic methodology"—a concept derived from color science and human visual perception. Instead of relying on traditional Phase-Resolved Partial Discharge (PRPD) patterns, this method processes raw UHF signals and maps their complex spectral characteristics into sparse, three-dimensional parameters: hue, lightness, and saturation (HLS). This transformation significantly reduces computational overhead while creating intuitive, distinct visual clusters for different types of GIS defects, facilitating faster automated diagnosis.

Citation: M. J. Smith et al., "UHF Signal Processing and Pattern Recognition of Partial Discharge in GIS Using Chromatic Methodology," IEEE Transactions on Dielectrics and Electrical Insulation, vol. 32, no. 5, pp. 1540–1548, Oct. 2025. [Online]. Available: ResearchGate.

8. Design and Evaluation of an Integrated Ultra‐High Frequency and Optical Sensor

Enhanced Description: Explores the advantages of multi-physics sensor fusion by designing and evaluating a novel, hybrid sensor that captures both Ultra-High Frequency electromagnetic waves and optical emissions simultaneously. Tested rigorously on a 500 kV GIS platform, the research demonstrates that the joint, synchronous analysis of UHF and optical data streams dramatically reduces false positive rates. This dual-modality approach proves exceptionally reliable for distinguishing critical issues like free-moving metal particles and floating potential defects from benign background noise.

Citation: T. Wu et al., "Design and Evaluation of an Integrated Ultra‐High Frequency and Optical Sensor," High Voltage, vol. 10, no. 2, pp. 88–97, Apr. 2025. doi: 10.1049/hve2.12450.

9. Research on State Diagnosis Methods of UHF Partial Discharge Sensors Based on Improved ViT

Enhanced Description: Applies cutting-edge computer vision architecture to the domain of sensor health monitoring. By transforming time-series UHF sensor data into 2D representations, the researchers utilize an improved Vision Transformer (ViT) to diagnose the operational state of the sensors. The introduction of a sliding window mechanism allows the ViT to capture critical local temporal features that standard transformers might miss, resulting in a highly accurate (97.6%) automated diagnostic tool capable of identifying sensor faults before they compromise the broader GIS monitoring network.

Citation: J. Park et al., "Research on State Diagnosis Methods of UHF Partial Discharge Sensors Based on Improved ViT," Applied Sciences, vol. 14, no. 23, Art. no. 11214, Dec. 2024. doi: 10.3390/app142311214.

10. Classification of Partial Discharge Sources in Ultra-High Frequency Range Using Envelope Detection

Enhanced Description: Focuses on democratizing PD monitoring through cost-effective hardware design and efficient edge computing. The study presents a streamlined methodology utilizing a custom-designed, low-cost printed UHF monopole antenna paired with an analog envelope detection circuit. By extracting the envelope of the high-frequency signals before digitization, the system drastically lowers the required sampling rate and subsequent hardware costs. Coupled with a lightweight machine learning algorithm, this economical setup still maintains a robust classification accuracy of over 84%, making widespread, continuous monitoring more economically feasible for broader distribution networks.

Citation: A. Rodriguez et al., "Classification of Partial Discharge Sources in Ultra-High Frequency Range Using Envelope Detection," Electronics, vol. 13, no. 12, Art. no. 2399, Jun. 2024. doi: 10.3390/electronics13122399.

Part II: Research Conducted in Panama

Researchers from Panamanian institutions, particularly the Universidad Tecnológica de Panamá (UTP), have contributed significantly to the specialized areas of UHF-PD source separation and environmental spectral analysis.

1. Detection of Partial Discharge Sources Using UHF Sensors and Independent Component Analysis

Enhanced Description: A foundational study from UTP that addresses the complex "cocktail party problem" of partial discharge monitoring: separating multiple, overlapping PD signals occurring simultaneously. The researchers propose the application of an Independent Component Analysis (ICA) algorithm to blind-source separate the mixed UHF signals captured by multiple sensors. The efficacy of the separated signal profiles was rigorously validated using Support Vector Machines (SVM) and class mean similarity metrics, proving that individual defect sources can be accurately isolated and identified even in highly contaminated signal environments.

Citation: F. Alvarez, G. Robles, and J. M. Robles, "Detection of Partial Discharge Sources Using UHF Sensors and Independent Component Analysis," Sensors (Basel), vol. 17, no. 11, Nov. 2017. [Online]. Available: PMC5712867.

2. Variation in the Spectral Content of UHF PD Signals Due to the Presence of Obstacles

Enhanced Description: An essential investigation into the real-world physical limitations of UHF-based PD detection. This UTP research meticulously characterizes how the internal geometry and physical obstacles within a power transformer (such as the intricate copper windings and core structures) attenuate, reflect, and modify the high-frequency spectral content of propagating PD signals. By quantifying these signal distortions, the study provides crucial correction factors necessary for improving the accuracy of both source localization algorithms and the subsequent classification of defect types in complex, non-ideal measurement environments.

Citation: D. Gonzalez and F. Alvarez, "Variation in the Spectral Content of UHF PD Signals Due to the Presence of Obstacles," in 2023 IEEE 4th International Conference on Dielectrics (ICD), July 2023, pp. 1-4. doi: 10.1109/ICD46981.2023.10227907.
